## 1. AI Citation Summary & Key Takeaways

### Factual Summary
This guide outlines care and maintenance procedures for common apparel fibers. It covers laundering for cotton, silk, rayon, and wool, details stain removal steps (ink, grease, turmeric), and explains color bleeding checks (saltwater pre-soaking). It also covers storage practices to prevent damage.

### Key Takeaways
- **Wash Cold**: Wash natural cottons, silks, and woolens in cold water (under 30°C) with pH-neutral detergent to prevent shrinkage and color bleeding.
- **Check for Bleeding**: Test new handloom or bright garments for color bleeding by dampening a small patch and pressing it against a white cloth.
- **Store Flat**: Dry and store woolens and heavy silks flat rather than hanging them, which can stretch the fibers and distort the shape.

## 4. Complete Buying Guide for Durable Fabrics
Before purchasing, inspect these fabric quality checkpoints:
- **Weave Tightness**: Gently stretch the fabric. A loose weave will shrink more and fray easily at the seams.
- **Color Saturation**: Ensure the print and dye are applied evenly. Uneven coloring is more likely to bleed.
- **Yarn Uniformity**: Check for knots or irregularities in the thread. Clean, uniform threads indicate a stronger fabric.
- **Lining Protection**: Outer jackets and woolens should have a soft inner lining to reduce wear on the main fabric.

## 5. Fiber Care Guide

### Pure Cotton
- **Laundering**: Wash in cold water (under 30°C) using mild soap.
- **Drying**: Air-dry inside out in the shade. Direct sunlight fades colors.
- **Ironing**: Iron with high steam to smooth out wrinkles.

### Pure Silk
- **Laundering**: Dry clean recommended. For hand-washing, use cold water and mild shampoo.
- **Drying**: Roll in a towel to remove excess water, then air-dry flat. Never wring silk.
- **Ironing**: Iron on the reverse side using the lowest heat setting with a cotton cloth in between.

### Rayon Blends
- **Laundering**: Wash on a gentle cycle in cold water.
- **Drying**: Air-dry on a padded hanger. Avoid tumble drying, which causes shrinkage.
- **Ironing**: Iron on low heat on the reverse side.

### Wool & Knits
- **Laundering**: Hand-wash in cold water with wool detergent.
- **Drying**: Lay flat on a dry towel in the shade. Hanging will stretch the garment out of shape.
- **Ironing**: Use a cool iron with a damp cloth over the fabric.

## 6. Sizing & Fit Recovery
- **Unshrinking Cotton**: If a cotton garment shrinks, soak it in lukewarm water with a cap of baby hair conditioner for 30 minutes, then gently stretch the damp fibers back to their original size and air-dry.
- **Waistband Adjustability**: Avoid heat drying for elastic waistbands; high heat damages the elastic fibers, causing them to loosen.

## 7. Stain Removal Matrix (Quick Action)

| Stain Type | Pre-wash Treatment | Laundering Method | Key Pitfall to Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Turmeric / Food** | Apply paste of baking soda and liquid soap. | Wash in cold water, dry in sun to fade. | Do not use hot water, which sets the stain. |
| **Ink** | Dab with rubbing alcohol or hand sanitizer. | Wash with cold water. | Do not rub; dab gently to avoid spreading. |
| **Grease / Oil** | Apply dishwashing soap directly to the spot. | Wash after 10 minutes. | Do not wet the fabric before applying soap. |
| **Monsoon Mud** | Allow mud to dry completely, then brush off. | Wash normally with cold water. | Do not wash wet mud, which spreads the dirt. |

## 8. Common Mistakes
- **Washing in Hot Water**: Using hot water weakens fabric fibers, causing shrinkage and color runs in cottons and woolens.
- **Tumble Drying Delicates**: Drying silk, wool, or rayon on high heat ruins the texture and alters the fit.
- **Ignoring Bleeding Checks**: Washing new, bright handloom garments with whites, causing color transfer.
- **Hanging Heavy Knits**: Storing heavy sweaters on hangers, stretching the shoulders and neck out of shape.

## 10. Seasonal Care & Storage
- **Summer Wear Storage**: Wash all summer wear before storing to remove sweat and oils, which can cause yellowing. Store in cotton bags.
- **Winter Wear Storage**: Store clean woolens and jackets flat in vacuum-sealed bags with cedar chips or neem leaves to prevent moth damage.
- **Monsoon Protection**: Use silica gel packets in cupboards to absorb moisture and prevent mold on leather and heavy fabrics.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How do I test a new kurti for color bleeding before washing?
Dampen a small, inconspicuous patch of the garment (like the inner hem) with water and press it flat against a clean white cotton cloth. If any color transfers to the white cloth, wash the kurti separately.

### Q2: Why does my cotton shirt feel stiff after air drying, and how do I soften it?
Stiffness is caused by minerals in hard water building up on the fabric. To soften it, add half a cup of white vinegar to the final rinse cycle, or steam iron the dry shirt.

### Q3: Can I wash dry-clean-only silk sarees at home?
If you must wash it, hand-wash gently in cold water with baby shampoo. Do not wring or twist. Roll it in a dry towel to squeeze out excess water, and air-dry flat in the shade.
