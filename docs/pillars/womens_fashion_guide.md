### Key Takeaways

- **Fabric Weight Mapping**: Choose lightweight cotton and rayon blends (90–120 GSM) for summer airflow; select heavier materials (180+ GSM) for winter layering.
- **Separation Laundering**: Handloom cottons and bright kurtas bleed dye during early washes. Wash separately in cold water with pH-neutral soap to preserve colors.

## 4. Complete Buying Guide
Check these specific quality checkpoints before purchasing:
- **Inner Interlocking Seams**: Turn the kurti inside out. Seams must be interlocked to prevent fabric fraying after machine washes.
- **Embroidery Backing**: Rub the back of any embroidery. Stiff, rough backing will irritate the skin. Look for soft fabric backings.
- **Placket Stitching**: The front button or zipper placket should lie completely flat without puckering.
- **Seam Margins**: Feel the side seams. Ensure there is enough spare fabric (at least 1.5 inches) to allow waist and bust adjustments as body shapes change.

## 5. Fabric Guide

### Pure Cotton
- **Benefits**: High moisture absorption, soft on skin, completely breathable.
- **Delhi Climate**: The absolute standard for Delhi summers (May to September) when humidity and dry heat peak.
- **GSM Specs**: 85 GSM to 115 GSM for hot weather; heavier cotton khadi (140-170 GSM) for autumn.
- **Care**: Wash in cold water and iron while slightly damp.

### Rayon Blends
- **Benefits**: Smooth drape, resists creases, feels cool to the touch.
- **Delhi Climate**: Highly popular during humid monsoon months (July to September) due to fast-drying properties.
- **GSM Specs**: 110 GSM to 130 GSM.
- **Care**: Avoid wringing tightly; air-dry on a hanger.

### Chanderi & Cotton-Silk
- **Benefits**: Subtle sheen, structured drape, looks elegant without excessive weight.
- **Delhi Climate**: Ideal for evening wear, festive gatherings, and wedding coordinates.
- **Care**: Dry clean or hand-wash gently with mild shampoo.

## 6. Size & Fit Guide

### Kurti & Suit Set Sizing Grid (Inches)

| Size Label | Chest Width | Waist Width | Hip Width | Shoulder Width | Length (Standard) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S** | 36" | 32" | 38" | 14.0" | 40" - 42" |
| **M** | 38" | 34" | 40" | 14.5" | 40" - 42" |
| **L** | 40" | 36" | 42" | 15.0" | 42" - 44" |
| **XL** | 42" | 38" | 44" | 15.5" | 42" - 44" |
| **XXL** | 44" | 40" | 46" | 16.0" | 44" |

### Sizing and Fit Pitfalls
- **Hip Measurement**: In straight-cut kurtis, a tight hip fit causes the side slits to rip under strain. Always size by the largest measurement (bust or hip).
- **Armhole Comfort**: If the armhole feels tight when reaching forward, the bust size is too small.

## 7. Seasonal Advice
- **Summer (April - July)**: Sleeveless or half-sleeve pure cotton kurtas in pastel shades (pink, mint, mint green, peach) matching white cotton trousers.
- **Monsoon (August - September)**: Rayon or cotton-poly blends that dry quickly. Darker colors prevent visible splash marks from muddy roads.
- **Winter (November - February)**: Cotton-silk suit sets layered with woolen shawls, cardigans, or warm innerwear.
- **Wedding/Festive Season**: Heavily coordinated Anarkali or straight suit sets in deep jewel tones (maroon, navy, emerald green).

---

## 8. Common Mistakes
- **Ignoring the Hip Fit**: Standardizing coordinates by chest size only, causing trousers or straight kurtas to pull tight across the hips.
- **Ignoring Shrinkage in Cotton**: Cotton suit sets shrink slightly after washing. Buying a tight fit leaves no room for comfort post-wash.
- **Washing Dark & Light Clothes Together**: Red, indigo, and black handblock prints bleed color. Washing them together will stain white bottoms.

---

## 9. Tailoring & Alteration Advice
Off-the-rack garments are designed for average proportions, but alterations ensure a proper fit:
- **Neckline Adjustments**: If a neckline is too deep, adding fabric inserts or darts prevents slipping.
- **Side Slit Reinforcement**: Ask your tailor to add a small double-stitch bar tack at the top of the side slits to prevent tearing.
- **Timelines**: Standard fittings and side-slit reinforcements at our Mubarakpur Road location take 24 hours. Full suit restructuring takes 48 hours.

---

## 10. Care & Maintenance
- **Handloom Outfits**: Soaking in saltwater for 10 minutes before the first wash helps lock in dyes and prevents future bleeding.
- **Indigo & Red Prints**: Always wash separately in cold water. Air-dry flat in the shade, away from direct sunlight.
- **Ironing**: Iron on the reverse side of printed or embroidered kurtas to prevent damage.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Why do my kurti side slits tear easily, and how can I prevent it?
Slits tear when the garment is too tight across the hips. When you sit, stress concentrates at the top of the slit. Ensure a relaxed fit and ask for a double-stitch reinforcing block at the top of the slit.

### Q2: How do I wash a new indigo kurti without fading?
Wash it separately in cold water with mild liquid soap. Avoid rubbing or soaking for long periods, and dry inside out in the shade.

### Q3: What is the correct length for straight pants?
Straight pants should end just above the ankle bone. This prevents the hem from dragging and stays clean on dusty streets.
