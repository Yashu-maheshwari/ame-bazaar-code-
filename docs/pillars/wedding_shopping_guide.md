## 4. Complete Buying Guide
Inspect these quality checkpoints before purchasing wedding wear:
- **Embroidery Backing**: Check the reverse side of heavy gold embroidery. It should have a soft lining to prevent skin irritation.
- **Seam Allowances**: Verify that all lehenga blouses and sherwanis have at least 2 inches of side-seam margin to allow for adjustments.
- **Zipper & Clasp Strength**: Heavy skirts require heavy-duty metal zippers with safety hooks at the top.
- **Drape Flexibility**: Hold the dupatta; it should be soft enough to pleat and pin without feeling stiff or heavy.

## 5. Fabric & Weight Guide

### Raw Silk & Brocade
- **Benefits**: Rich texture, natural sheen, holds structured shapes well.
- **Wedding Suitability**: Standard choice for winter weddings and formal groom wear.
- **Care**: Dry clean only; store wrapped in cotton.

### Georgette & Net
- **Benefits**: Soft drape, matches body contours easily, holds embroidery cleanly.
- **Wedding Suitability**: Ideal for bridal lehengas, dupattas, and pre-wedding event coordinates.
- **Care**: Hang on padded hangers to prevent stretching.

### Satin & Crepe (for Linings)
- **Benefits**: Smooth, prevents friction, protects skin from outer embellishments.
- **Wedding Suitability**: Essential inner layer for all heavy bridal wear.
- **Care**: Hand wash or dry clean.

### Wedding Sizing & Alteration Timelines

| Event Phase | Timing Target | Fit Verification Goal | Key Checkpoint |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Booking** | 90 Days Before | Select base size, check shoulder alignments. | Verify 2" side margins. |

## 7. Seasonal Wedding Advice
- **Summer Weddings (April - July)**: Light pastel georgette, crepe, or cotton-silk coordinates. Choose peach, mint green, and cream to stay cool.
- **Winter Weddings (November - February)**: Heavy raw silk, velvet, or brocade coordinates in deep jewel tones (maroon, navy, emerald green).
- **Pre-Wedding Functions (Haldi / Mehendi)**: Lightweight, breathable cotton or rayon coordinates that are easy to wash.

---

## 8. Common Mistakes
- **Last-Minute Alterations**: Leaving final fittings for the week of the wedding, leaving no time for corrections.
- **Incorrect Shoe Measurement**: Hemming skirts or trousers without wearing the actual shoes planned for the wedding.
- **Ignoring Inner Linings**: Buying heavy outfits with scratchy synthetic linings that cause skin irritation during long hours of wear.

---

## 9. Tailoring & Alteration Advice
- **Dupatta Pinning Adjustments**: Adding small loops inside the shoulder seam helps secure the dupatta in place.
- **Can-Can Adjustments**: If the skirt is too stiff or heavy, your tailor can remove layers of can-can netting to improve comfort.
- **Timelines**: Premium wedding alterations at our Mubarakpur Road store are completed within 48 to 72 hours, with express 24-hour fittings available for out-of-town guests.

## 10. Care & Maintenance
- **Dry Cleaning**: Always dry clean heavy wedding wear after use to remove sweat, food, or dirt stains.
- **Storage**: Store heavy bridal wear and sherwanis flat in breathable cotton garment bags. Never hang heavy garments, which can stretch and damage the fabric.
- **Ironing**: Use a professional steam press; never use a dry iron directly on embroidery or silk.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How early should I buy and alter my wedding outfit?
We recommend purchasing your outfit 90 days before the wedding. Complete the first fitting 45 days out, and perform the final fitting exactly 30 days before the wedding to accommodate weight shifts.

### Q2: Why is my lehenga skirt dragging even though the length was measured?
This occurs when the length is measured without wearing the actual wedding shoes. Always bring your wedding shoes to every fitting session.

### Q3: How do I prevent my heavy bridal dupatta from pulling on my hair?
Use a double-pinning method. Secure the dupatta to your hair with bobby pins, and use a safety pin to secure the fabric to the shoulder seam of your blouse to distribute the weight.
