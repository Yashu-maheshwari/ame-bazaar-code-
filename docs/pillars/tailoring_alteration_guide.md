## 4. Complete Buying Guide for Tailored Fits
Inspect these checkpoints to ensure a garment can be altered properly:
- **Inner Margins**: Turn the shirt or kurti inside out. Ensure there is enough spare fabric (at least 1.5 inches) along the side seams to allow for let-out adjustments.
- **Armhole Seams**: Armhole adjustments are complex. If the armhole feels tight or constricting, try one size larger and alter the waist instead.
- **Border Designs**: For sarees or kurtas with elaborate borders, ensure the border is stitched on separately; this allows the hem to be adjusted without cutting into the pattern.
- **Thread Match**: Ensure the alteration tailor uses matching thread colors and weights to maintain the garment's original look.

## 5. Fit Classification Guide

### Comfort Fit
- **Benefits**: Loose drape, provides ease of movement, sits comfortably on the body.
- **When to Choose**: Daily wear, warm weather, and active tasks.
- **Alteration Specs**: Leaves 3 to 4 inches of extra fabric around the chest and waist relative to actual body measurements.

### Slim Fit
- **Benefits**: Modern silhouette, sits closer to the body, clean lines.
- **When to Choose**: Formal office wear and social events.
- **Alteration Specs**: Leaves 1.5 to 2 inches of ease around the waist and chest.

## 6. Sizing & Body Measurement Guide
Follow these measurement guidelines using a flexible tape measure:

### Body Measurement Guidelines (Inches)

| Measurement Type | How to Measure | Key Fit Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Chest / Bust** | Wrap tape around the fullest part of chest, keep tape level under armpits. | Hold tape flat but comfortable. |
| **Waist** | Measure at the natural waistline where trousers sit. | Keep a one-finger space under tape. |
| **Shoulder Yoke** | Measure from outer shoulder bone to outer shoulder bone. | Align seam with bone edge. |
| **Inseam** | Measure inner leg from crotch seam to trouser ankle hem. | Medium break rests on shoe laces. |
| **Sleeve Length** | Measure from shoulder seam to wrist bone. | Cuff should end at thumb base. |

## 7. Seasonal & Event Fitting Advice
- **Summer (April - August)**: Keep fits slightly loose to allow airflow. Tight synthetic fits cause sweat accumulation and skin irritation.
- **Winter (November - February)**: Alter outerwear (coats, jackets) while wearing a sweater or thermal layer to ensure comfortable fit.
- **Festive & Wedding Fit**: Ensure traditional trousers have enough rise room for sitting cross-legged during puja services.

---

## 8. Common Fitting Mistakes
- **Sizing to the Tag**: Assuming your size is the same across all brands. Always try the garment on or check the physical measurements.
- **Ignoring the Seam Margins**: Buying cheap garments with raw, un-interlocked seams that fray when let out.
- **Altering the Wrong Area**: Trying to alter a tight shoulder. Shoulders are difficult to adjust; buy for the shoulders and alter the waist instead.

## 10. Care & Maintenance of Altered Clothes
- **Avoid Pulling Threads**: If an alteration thread becomes loose, snip it with scissors; do not pull, which can unravel the seam.
- **Pressing**: Press seams flat after washing to maintain the altered shape of the garment.
- **Laundering**: Wash altered cottons in cold water to prevent uneven shrinkage along the new stitch lines.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can a tight shirt be let out?
Only if the garment has sufficient inner side-seam margins. Check inside the shirt; if there is at least 1 inch of spare fabric on each side, the shirt can be let out.

### Q2: What does "single break" mean for trousers?
A single break means the trouser hem rests lightly on the top of your shoe, creating a single soft fold in the fabric. This keeps the hem clean and off the ground.

### Q3: Why are shoulder alterations so expensive?
Shoulder alterations require removing the sleeves, recutting the armhole, and reattaching the sleeve crown. This is a skilled process that takes time to align properly.
