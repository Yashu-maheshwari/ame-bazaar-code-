## 4. Complete Buying Guide
Inspect these quality checkmarks when purchasing seasonal wear:
- **Weave Openness**: Hold summer fabrics up to the light. A looser weave lets air flow, keeping you cooler in hot weather.
- **Seam Stitching**: Winter garments should have dense, tight seams to keep cold wind out.
- **Zippers & Fasteners**: Winter jackets and raincoats need rust-resistant alloy or plastic zippers that won't seize up in damp weather.
- **Lining Quality**: Ensure winter coats have a soft inner lining that prevents friction with your clothes.

## 6. Sizing & Fit Guide for Layering
- **Thermal Layering Space**: When buying winter shirts or jackets, choose a half-size larger to leave room for thermal undershirts without binding under the arms.
- **Summer Fit**: A loose comfort fit (leaving 3 to 4 inches of ease around the chest and waist) is essential in summer to allow air circulation and sweat evaporation.

## 7. Seasonal Clothing Recommendations
- **Summer (April - July)**: Pastel cotton kurtas, linen shirts, and white cotton trousers coordinates. Light shades reflect sunlight.
- **Monsoon (August - September)**: Rayon suit sets and synthetic blend pants. Avoid heavy denim, which stays damp and heavy when wet.
- **Winter (November - February)**: Thermal base layers under flannel shirts and wool-blend sweaters or jackets.
- **Festival / Wedding Season (October - January)**: Mid-weight cotton-silk kurtas and lined ethnic coordinates.

## 8. Common Mistakes
- **Wearing Heavy Synthetics in Summer**: Choosing cheap polyester shirts that trap heat and sweat, causing body odor and skin rashes.
- **Ignoring GSM Labels**: Buying heavy denim or thick cotton shirts for summer because the color looks good.
- **Storing Damp Winter Wear**: Storing winter coats and blankets before they are completely dry, leading to mold and mildew.

## 10. Care & Maintenance
- **Seasonal Storage**: Clean all winter clothes before storing them. Store in vacuum bags with silica packets and cedar blocks to repel pests.
- **Monsoon Drying**: Always dry clothes completely in a well-ventilated space. Avoid leaving damp clothes in a heap.
- **Ironing**: Use appropriate heat settings: high steam for linen, medium for cotton, and low heat for synthetics.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: What is GSM, and why does it matter for summer clothes?
GSM stands for Grams per Square Meter. It measures fabric density. A lower GSM (80–110) means the fabric is lightweight and breathable, which is ideal for hot summers.

### Q2: How do I layer clothes for Delhi winters without looking bulky?
Use a thin, snug-fitting thermal base layer, followed by a mid-weight cotton shirt (130-150 GSM), and top with a wool-blend sweater or jacket. This traps heat in layers without adding bulk.

### Q3: How do I store woolens to prevent moth damage?
Clean all woolen garments to remove sweat and oils before storing. Place them in airtight cotton or vacuum bags with cedar wood chips or dried neem leaves to repel pests.
