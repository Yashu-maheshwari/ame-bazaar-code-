## 4. Complete Buying Guide
Inspect these quality checkmarks before purchasing festival wear:
- **Lining Quality**: Heavy ethnic wear should have soft cotton lining (mulmul) inside to prevent itching from embroidery threads.
- **Weave Density**: Hold fabric to the light; a tight weave is less flammable than a loose, open weave.
- **Border Stitching**: Borders should be sewn flat with double stitching to prevent them from catching on objects.
- **Sleeve Fittings**: Select buttoned cuffs or snug sleeve ends for active kitchen or puja tasks, avoiding loose, draping sleeves.

## 5. Fabric Guide

### Mid-Weight Cotton
- **Benefits**: Breathable, comfortable in heat, and naturally fire-resistant.
- **Festival Suitability**: Ideal for Diwali pujas and hot summer festivals.
- **GSM Specs**: 120 GSM to 150 GSM.
- **Care**: Wash in cold water and iron with heavy steam.

### Cotton-Silk Blends
- **Benefits**: Subtle sheen, holds drape well, and feels soft against the skin.
- **Festival Suitability**: Perfect for Eid and evening festival gatherings.
- **GSM Specs**: 110 GSM to 130 GSM.
- **Care**: Dry clean or hand-wash gently with mild liquid soap.

### Rayon Blends
- **Benefits**: Smooth drape, crease-resistant, and dries quickly.
- **Festival Suitability**: Great for casual daytime visits.
- **Care**: Avoid wringing tightly; air-dry on a hanger.

## 7. Seasonal Festival Advice
- **Summer Festivals (Eid, Teej)**: Lightweight cotton-silk blends or soft cotton coordinates. Choose pastels and light shades to stay cool in the sun.
- **Autumn/Winter Festivals (Diwali, Chhath Puja)**: Layered ethnic wear, heavy cotton kurtas, and silk-blend sarees coordinates. Warm shawls or jackets can be added for evening events.
- **Monsoon Festivals (Rakhi)**: Rayon blends that dry quickly if caught in sudden rain.

---

## 8. Common Mistakes
- **Buying Flammable Synthetics for Pujas**: Selecting cheap polyester or nylon garments that melt easily when exposed to fire sparks from oil lamps.
- **Loose, Hanging Sleeve Styles**: Wear garments with wide, hanging sleeves near cooking areas or oil lamps.
- **Ignoring Color Bleeding**: Washing bright red or blue festive clothes with other laundry, causing stains.

---

## 9. Tailoring & Alteration Advice
- **Sleeve Tapering**: Tapering loose sleeves keeps them out of danger near flames.
- **Fast Alteration Timelines**: Alteration requests increase before major festivals. Simple adjustments are completed in 24 hours at our Mubarakpur Road store.
- **Double Stitching**: Reinforcing seams on kids' ethnic outfits prevents rips during active play.

## 10. Care & Maintenance
- **Dry Cleaning**: Dry clean silk and heavily embroidered garments to prevent water damage and color run.
- **Stain Treatment**: For Holi stains, soak the white cotton garment in cold water with lemon juice or white vinegar before washing.
- **Storage**: Store festive wear in zip-lock cotton bags with silica gel packets to absorb humidity.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: What fabrics are safest to wear near festive oil lamps (diyas)?
Pure cotton and cotton-silk blends are the safest choices. They have high ignition points and do not melt when exposed to sparks, unlike synthetic polyester or nylon.

### Q2: How can I coordinate my family's outfits without wearing identical colors?
Choose a shared accent color. If the father wears a cream kurta with a maroon pocket square, the mother can wear a maroon saree, and the children can wear cream outfits with maroon borders.

### Q3: How do I remove turmeric (haldi) stains from cotton clothes?
Apply a paste of liquid detergent and baking soda to the stain, let it sit for 15 minutes, wash in cold water, and air-dry in direct sunlight to fade the stain.
