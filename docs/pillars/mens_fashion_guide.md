## 4. Complete Buying Guide
When evaluating a garment in the hand, check these specific quality indicators:
- **Stitching Quality**: Look closely at the seams. A high-quality shirt has 16 to 18 stitches per inch. Loose, wide stitching (under 10 stitches per inch) will fray under stress.
- **Buttonholes & Buttons**: Buttonholes should have dense stitching with no loose threads. Check if the buttons are cross-stitched for durability.
- **Hem Integrity**: Trousers should have a double-folded hem. Pull gently on the seams; if the fabric separates easily, the weave is too loose.
- **Fabric Weight**: Hold the garment up to the light. A dense weave indicates stronger threads that resist tearing at the elbows or knees.

## 5. Fabric Guide
Understanding what you are wearing makes a difference in comfort and durability.

### Cotton
- **Benefits**: Soft, absorbs sweat, and does not irritate the skin.
- **When to Wear**: Ideal for daily wear, outdoor commutes, and casual outings.
- **Delhi Weather Suitability**: Essential during the summer heat (April to September) when temperatures exceed 40°C.
- **GSM & Technical Specs**: Look for lightweight cottons between 90 GSM and 120 GSM for summer comfort. Heavier oxfords (150-180 GSM) are better suited for mild autumn days.
- **Care**: Wash in cold water (under 30°C) to prevent the natural fibers from tightening.

### Linen
- **Benefits**: Highly breathable, has an open weave, and dries quickly.
- **When to Wear**: Semi-formal gatherings and warm evening social events.
- **Delhi Weather Suitability**: Excellent for dry heat and humid monsoon months.
- **GSM Specs**: Typically ranges from 110 GSM to 140 GSM.
- **Care**: Iron while still damp to easily remove characteristic wrinkles.

### Cotton-Polyester Blends
- **Benefits**: Resists wrinkles, retains dye color, and requires minimal ironing.
- **When to Wear**: Daily office wear and long commutes.
- **Delhi Weather Suitability**: Suitable for year-round office environments.
- **Care**: Can be machine washed and dries quickly.

## 6. Size & Fit Guide
Relying on size tags is the most common cause of poor fit. Standardize your measurements:

### Standard Sizing Grid (Inches)

| Size Label | Chest Width | Shoulder Yoke | Regular Sleeve | Torso Length |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S** | 36" - 38" | 17.0" | 24.5" | 28.5" |
| **M** | 39" - 41" | 17.5" | 25.0" | 29.0" |
| **L** | 42" - 44" | 18.0" | 25.5" | 30.0" |
| **XL** | 45" - 47" | 19.0" | 26.0" | 31.0" |

### Fitting Observations
- **Shoulder Seam**: The shoulder seam must rest exactly on the edge of the shoulder bone. If it droops over the arm, the shirt is too large; if it pulls tight across the back, it is too small.
- **Comfort vs. Slim Fit**: A comfort fit leaves 3 to 4 inches of extra fabric around the waist for ease of movement. A slim fit reduces this to 1.5 to 2 inches, sitting closer to the body.

## 7. Seasonal Advice
- **Summer (April - August)**: Wear lightweight, loose cotton and linen shirts (90-110 GSM). Choose light colors like beige, sky blue, and white to reflect heat.
- **Monsoon (August - September)**: Opt for cotton-poly blends that dry quickly and resist mildew. Avoid heavy denim that takes hours to dry.
- **Winter (November - February)**: Layer with thermal undershirts, heavy twill shirts (180 GSM), and wool-blend sweaters or jackets.
- **Wedding/Festival Season (October - January)**: Traditional kurtas and churidar sets. Ensure the kurta has side pockets and is made of breathable cotton-silk blends.

## 8. Common Mistakes
- **Buying by Tag Alone**: Different brands have different sizing patterns. Always try the garment or measure it.
- **Ignoring Shrinkage**: Unwashed 100% cotton shrinks during the first wash. If a raw cotton shirt fits tight in the store, it will be too small after washing.
- **Choosing the Wrong Rise**: Buying low-rise trousers when you need to sit or bend frequently during work leads to constant pulling and discomfort.
- **Ignoring Fabric Bleeding**: Buying bright handloom kurtas and washing them with whites will ruin other clothes.

## 9. Tailoring & Alteration Advice
Many off-the-rack clothes do not fit perfectly. Alterations make a garment look custom-made.
- **Sleeve Length**: Sleeves should end at the base of the thumb. Adjusting them is a simple alteration.
- **Trouser Hems**: Adjusting the hem to sit with a "single break" on the shoe keeps trousers from dragging on dusty roads.
- **Timelines**: Simple hemming and waist alterations at our Mubarakpur Road store are completed within 24 hours. Complex shoulder adjustments take up to 48 hours.

---

## 10. Care & Maintenance
- **Cotton Shirts**: Turn inside out before washing to protect buttons and outer fibers. Use cold water.
- **Trousers**: Wash after 2-3 wears to maintain shape. Air-dry away from direct hot sunlight to prevent fading.
- **Ethnic Kurtas**: Hand wash separately in cold water for the first three washes to check for dye bleeding.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How do I know if a shirt collar fits correctly?
You should be able to slide two fingers comfortably between the buttoned collar and your neck. If you cannot fit two fingers, the collar is too tight and will cause discomfort during hot Delhi days.

### Q2: What is the difference between inseam and rise?
The rise determines where the trousers sit on your hips or waist. The inseam measures the length along the inner leg from the crotch to the ankle hem.

### Q3: My trousers drag on the ground. What break should I request?
Ask for a "medium break" or "single break." The trouser hem will rest lightly on your shoe laces, keeping the back edge clear of the ground and preventing wear and tear.
