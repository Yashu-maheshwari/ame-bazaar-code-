## 4. Complete Buying Guide
Inspect these quality checkmarks before purchasing:
- **Weave Backing**: Turn the saree border over. Look for loose, hanging threads in the weave; dense, cleanly tucked threads indicate a durable weave that won't catch on jewelry.
- **Fabric Softness**: Rub the fabric between your hands. A good georgette or silk should drape smoothly without feeling stiff or scratchy.
- **Blouse Piece Length**: Ensure the included unstitched blouse piece is at least 80 cm long to allow for proper sizing and design adjustments.
- **Zari Quality**: Good quality zari borders contain fine metallic threads that do not tarnish or darken easily when exposed to air.

## 5. Fabric Guide

### Pure Cotton
- **Benefits**: Breathable, absorbs sweat, and provides a crisp, structured drape.
- **Delhi Climate**: Ideal for hot summer months.
- **Care**: Wash in cold water and iron with heavy steam.

### Georgette & Crepe
- **Benefits**: Soft drape, matches body contours easily, resists wrinkles.
- **Delhi Climate**: Highly popular during monsoon and humid autumn months due to its fast-drying, lightweight properties.
- **Care**: Hand wash gently and air-dry flat in the shade.

### Silk & Silk Blends
- **Benefits**: Natural sheen, rich texture, and holds structured pleats.
- **Delhi Climate**: The standard choice for autumn/winter weddings and festivals.
- **Care**: Dry clean only; store wrapped in cotton.

### Draping and Fit Tips
- **Shoulder Drop Prevention**: If the blouse shoulder straps slip, ask your tailor to add shoulder darts and internal snap-button loops to secure the bra straps.
- **Trouser-Length Alignment**: Wear your shoes or heels while measuring the saree length to ensure the hem sits just above the floor without dragging.

## 7. Seasonal Advice
- **Summer (April - July)**: Light pastel cotton or mulmul sarees in pastel colors (sky blue, light yellow, mint green).
- **Monsoon (August - September)**: Faux georgette or synthetic crepe sarees that dry quickly and don't stain easily from mud splashes.

## 8. Common Mistakes
- **Skipping Fall & Pico**: Draping a saree without attaching the cotton fall makes the bottom hem limp and prone to catching on shoes.
- **Buying Stiff Blouse Backing**: Stiff interfacing material inside the blouse neck causes itching and skin irritation.
- **Hanging Silk Sarees**: Hanging heavy silk sarees on metal hangers stretches the weave and distorts the shape. Always fold them.

---

## 9. Tailoring & Alteration Advice
- **Fall & Pico Finishing**: Adding a pre-washed cotton fall to the inner bottom edge prevents hem tears and adds weight to the drape.
- **Blouse Adjustments**: We add internal side-seam margins of 1.5 inches to all blouses, allowing them to be easily let out or taken in as needed.
- **Timelines**: Fall/pico finishing and simple blouse alterations at our Kirari store are completed in 24 hours. Full blouse tailoring takes 3 to 4 days.

---

## 10. Care & Maintenance
- **Silk Sarees**: Dry clean only. Never use hot irons directly on silk; always place a cotton cloth over the fabric.
- **Cotton Sarees**: Starch lightly during laundering to maintain a crisp, professional drape.
- **Storage**: Store sarees separately in soft cotton bags. Do not use plastic bags, which trap humidity and cause mildew.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: What is a saree fall, and why is it necessary?
A fall is a 3-meter strip of cotton fabric sewn onto the inner bottom hem of a saree. It provides weight to create clean, structured pleats and protects the hem from wear and tear.

### Q2: Why does my blouse shoulder keep slipping down?
This is typically caused by a loose bust line or shoulder straps that are too wide. Adding internal shoulder snaps that hook onto your bra straps prevents them from slipping.

### Q3: How do I store heavy silk sarees to prevent the metal threads from tarnishing?
Store silk sarees inside out in soft cotton bags. Change the folds every three to four months to prevent creasing and fiber breakage along the fold lines.
